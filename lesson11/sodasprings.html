Soda Springs Geyser

by John Doe

As many know, our city of Soda Springs is named for the hundreds of natural springs of carbonated water located in and around the city. These springs were well known to Native Americans and were famous among travelers headed west on the Oregon Trail in the 1800s. Did you know our town is also known for the Soda Springs Geyser? It was first discovered in 1934 when it was drilled into as people were looking for a hot pool attraction. The geyser initially ran for weeks, but eventually was capped off and only manually released for tourists to view. Now it is let loose every hour by a timed release valve. The size of the geyser has not decreased even after many years, and remains an eruption controlled geyser. Make sure to visit the viewing platforms where you can watch the geyser erupt!
